Smart Irrigation System – AI-Driven Agricultural Solution for Bangladesh

Project Overview: Water is the lifeline of agriculture, yet traditional irrigation in Bangladesh often leads to wastage and inefficiency. I designed a Smart Irrigation System combining soil moisture sensing, plant genetics, and AI to automate water usage, ensuring healthy crops while minimizing waste.

Problem: Bangladesh faces multiple agricultural challenges:

  • Water Waste: Overwatering due to uniform schedules wastes water and increases costs.
  • Drought & Scarcity: Water shortages reduce crop yield during dry seasons.
  • Economic & Environmental: Excessive water increases energy use, lowers income, and harms sustainability.

Solution: The AI-driven system solves these problems through:

  • Plant-Specific Watering: Each plant receives water based on its genetic and environmental needs.
  • Soil Moisture Sensing: Real-time sensors adjust irrigation automatically.
  • Water Conservation & Storage: Excess water is stored for drought periods, supporting sustainability.
  • AI Integration: The AI model learns from data to optimize irrigation efficiency over time.

Real-World Impact:

  • Economic: Reduces water and energy costs, optimizes crop yield, supports Bangladesh’s agricultural economy.
  • Environmental: Minimizes water waste, lowers carbon footprint.
  • Renewable Energy Potential: Stored water can generate electricity for farms and rural communities.

Future Work: Next phases include harnessing irrigation water for electricity, scaling to industrial farms, and integrating with solar-powered IoT systems for global applications.

Status: Completed
