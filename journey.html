2019 — 2026

Seven years.
One long build.

Every year added a layer — a new robot, a new competition, a new country, a new problem to solve. Here's how it stacked up, from a school line-follower to founding Miracle Robotics.

2019
The first robot

A line-follower, and the bug.

Started my robotics journey at school with a classic Line Following Robot (LFR) project. It worked. It was the first time I saw code make something move in the real world — and that was the bug. Everything since traces back to here.

  • First hardware build: sensors, motors, microcontroller
  • First taste of debugging in the physical world
2022
First system · First podium

Home automation, and a math medal.

Built an AI-driven system to automate every routine task at home — lights, fans, appliances. It became the seed of what would later evolve into the AI Smart Home product. Same year, secured 6th place in the national Mental Math Olympiad.

  • Home automation system shipped — running on real hardware
  • 🥉 6th place · National Mental Math Olympiad
2023
Bangladesh → Singapore

Representing the country at FIRST Global.

Won 2nd place in a national innovation contest focused on solving Bangladesh's daily problems. Then selected for the national team at the FIRST Global Challenge 2023 in Singapore — theme: Hydrogen Horizons. Built a robot tackling climate change. Came 36th out of 186 countries.

  • 🥈 2nd place · National innovation contest (Bangladesh)
  • 🌏 36 / 186 · FIRST Global Challenge, Singapore — Team Bangladesh
  • Theme: Hydrogen Horizons (climate change)
2024
MIT · Oxford · Olympiad

Going global, going deeper.

Climbed to 4th place in the Mental Math Olympiad. Joined the MIT-led summer robotics workshop "International Experience: The Leading Edge", learning advanced automation. Nominated for the Council of British International Schools award, and stepped into the Oxford Union debating chamber.

  • 🥇 4th place · National Mental Math Olympiad
  • 🎓 MIT summer robotics workshop — "International Experience: The Leading Edge"
  • 🏅 Nominated · Council of British International Schools award
  • 🎤 Participated · Oxford Union Debating Society
2025
Hanoi → Panama

A summit, and a second world stage.

Attended the Actions for Earth Global Youth Summit in Hanoi (July 2025) — collaborating with peers from across the world on SDG and climate-change solutions. Then represented Bangladesh again at FIRST Global Challenge 2025 in Panama under the theme Eco-Equilibrium, designing a robot for environmental resilience. Came 43rd out of 191 countries.

  • 🌏 Actions for Earth Global Youth Summit · Hanoi, Vietnam
  • 🌏 43 / 191 · FIRST Global Challenge, Panama — Team Bangladesh
  • Theme: Eco-Equilibrium (environmental resilience)
2026
Now · The company

Founded Miracle Robotics.

Took everything I'd built — the robots, the AI, the prototypes, the network — and turned it into a company. Miracle Robotics exists to build robotics and AI products as charity-aligned community solutions for Bangladesh and beyond. Six prototypes are ready. The next step is scale.

  • 🏢 Founded · Miracle Robotics
  • 🎯 Mission · charity-driven community tech solutions
  • 📦 6 investor-ready prototypes (see projects)
  • ⚡ Next: commercialise the AI Smart Irrigation system
What's next

The next chapter is yours to back.

The immediate goal: commercialise one of the existing prototypes — most likely the Smart Irrigation system — and continue developing new products that benefit communities. Tech for social impact is the through-line. Always has been.
